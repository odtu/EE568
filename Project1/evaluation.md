## Project Guideline

### Submission Rules:

- All projects files must be uploaded to your GitHub accounts, and the final reports must be submitted via ODTUClass.
- Reports submitted via email or in any physical form will not be accepted.
- Late submissions will not be accepted.
- All reports will be checked using Turnitin, and plagiarism will not be tolerated (remember, I have all the reports from the previous years).
- Hand written reports are not permitted.

### Report Quality Check:

An initial evaluation will be conducted after submission. If the report fails to meet the required standards, you will be given three days to revise and resubmit it, subject to a 15% penalty.

The report must satisfy the following criteria:

- Include a separate title page
- Contain an introduction that clearly outlines the objectives and scope of the report
- Be well-organized, with clearly defined sections and subsections
- Include properly labeled figures and tables with descriptive captions
- Present graphs with clearly labeled axes, appropriate legends, and readable font sizes
- Use high-quality figures with sufficient resolution

### Procedure:

You are encouraged to use professional tools such as LaTeX or MATLAB Report Generator; however, Microsoft Word is also acceptable (you need to upload your reports in PDF format).

If your analytical model is developed using a programming environment (e.g., MATLAB or Python), you must include the source code in your GitHub repository. Ensure that your code is well-structured, readable, and properly documented.

Material properties relevant to your selected case must be included in the appendix of your report.

### AI Usage:

You may use AI-based tools to support your work, provided that you fully understand and can explain all content included in your submission. Blindly copying AI-generated outputs is strongly discouraged.

In order to prevent that, I am planning to call around 25% of the students for oral explanation of their reports. If you fail to fully explain what you did in your reports, I will reduce your grade.

## Evaluation

The project will be evaluated using the following guideline:

**Number of Commits (15%):** I expect you have at least 3 commits on three different days. Each commit should include a clear and meaningful message. If you start early and continue editing your files, you’ll get full credit. The projects are time consuming, so this is a way to encourage you to start early and work regularly. 

**Technical Level (85%):** The level of detail of your simulations. Please give extra attention to your discussions and comparison sections. Please state any assumptions you make and discuss any discrepancies between different simulation approaches.
